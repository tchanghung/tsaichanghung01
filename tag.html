SIMPLE but RICH - Touch and go Coffee

" Delight your coffee time with more inpiration. "

Project Overview

2020.01~2020.04
Objective
Rich the experience of purchasing banners and arranging the lable with more interacitive and delightful user interface.
Deliverables
To reponse needs from our client and end-user, I designed a new label page with a intuitive user interface that inspires users when they tailor a original bottle for themselves. In addtion, support users smoothly go forward to explore more ideas on a delightful banner shop.
Chanllenge
Somehow there have already rather specific requirements (=limitations) from our clients, as it implied that how to visualize the concepts properly was quite important in this project.
Outcome
We created some new experience with more enjoyable elements that beyond our clien't requirements and, as we expected, TOUCH AND GO COFFEE went viral (ツイッターでバズった) again after thess new features released.

Background

Since Suntotry released α totally brand new service, TOUCH AND GO COFFEE, which based on our mobile ordering system from 2019/06, it became a big trend among teenagers, especially those who enthusiastic about the special activity in Tapan ”推し活”. Within this context, Suntory decided to conduct a customer-drawing tactic: Rich the cunstomize of the key value of this serived "Label" by 1) Enlarging the variety and quantity of banners, incluing paid-banner. 2) Providing the long-awaited feature: arrange tailor the "Label".

Define

Based on the existing elegant visual style, I decided to create a simple but enjoyable interface to keep not only the perception of the brand also the sensation.


購入体験を "Customer but FAST" から、さらに "SIMPLE but RICH" へ進化

Visible and Predictable. We assumpt that users visit the My Label page for seeking a awesome Label design that can represent his/her feeling at a certain present. Easy to adjust and always display the lastest design are necessary.

Delightful and Communicative. Inspiration and ideas always come from visual informaiton (stimuli).

より明確的な・安心的なオーダー体験

Setting a proper pickup time is rather important on this service because it represents a sign of when should start to prepare the meal and the deadline of the preparation task. During the usability testing, I found that almost of the participants didn't change the pickup time. Here were some reasons from them:

  • I did check the pickup time, and the default pickup time is good for me.
  • Oh, actually I didn't read it, I mean, I've never been aware of the pickup time.

I could be an crucial problem that caused they place an order that they can't pickup on time actually or an unitended pickup time.

Noticeable and clear pickup time information. By giving a particular visual weight of pickup time information and emphasizing the "The earliest estimated pickup time" by red color, I orginaized a new layout which would be ease to focus on and ease to understand the current state and make a decision.

Predictable and less cogonitive load. State the rule clearly and display the supporting text underneath the input rather than as a placeholder to avoid memorizing.

ライティング・言い回し

I've learned from the previous user research that users tended to omit some copies and respond quickly depending on if their mental model was strong enough or not. That means it could be more concise and help users focus on the content. However, sometimes users will feel slightly confused even if they knew there was only one way to go forward. And I solved this kind of problems by conducting several user testing to discover their mental model.

Concise and communicative copy. Tell users the only important things with visual weight.

Action-oriented copy. Trigger users' motivation and emotion by specific object and navigate users to where they should go with predictable words.

Evaluate

Since the primary goal of this project was improve the efficiency of the existing ordering experience. Does it work? I conducted an evaluation both on the redesign and the old one to compare the efficiency, which includes:

How much time would be saved while ordering on the redesign? How fast will users complete the ordering task compare with the current version?

To answer the question above, I ran a summative usability testing both on existing one and the redesign.

Comparing with the existing version, here comes the significant outcome when 32 participants completed the tasks on the redesign as below:

1/3
⏱TIME SAVED
3/4
👍ERRORS* REDUCED

* Errors is refered to 1) Slips and 2) Mistakes

Summary of this evaluation

  • Goal
    1. Assess the time on task of "Choose and add items to cart" on both the redesign and old version
    2. Assess the accuracy of "Choose and add items to cart" on both the redesign and old version
    3. Check the whole ordering usability on the redesign
  • Participants
    1. 32 (two of them was in pilot testing, 27 of them have pioir experience of the old site)
  • Methodology
    1. Usability testing
    2. Within-subject design (all of the participants went through the task on new and old one but with different order: half of participants went from new to old, while the others went the opposite order)
  • Task
    1. "Order four items which exist in different four kinds of categories and add them to the cart"
    2. "Imagine you feel like go for Marugame for lunch (or dinner, depends on when the test took place) first. What would you want to eat? Now you can order-ahead and complete the payment on your smartphone, and pick it up later without wasting your time, please order what you want and tell me if you think done."
  • Result
    1. Average time on task1: Redesign: 55 sec. The Old site: 78 sec.
    2. 100% of participants complete the task1 on the redersign faster than on the old one (even most of them have piror experience on the old site)　
    3. Accuracy*: Redesign→ Level3: 100%; the old site→ Level3: 23%, Level2: 53%, Level1: 23%
    4. Satisfation: Redesign > the old site: 27; Redesign ≥ the old site: 3

*Definition of Accuracy in this study: Level3: Zero time waste (or only slip one time); Level2: Repeatedly slips on the same step; Level1: Many mistakes happen and waste much time for the unintentional action.
